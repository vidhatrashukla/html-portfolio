# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Recruiters doing a quick background check, people reverting to a cold email, hiring managers who landed here from a resume link. Primary behaviour: scan for 30–60 seconds, decide whether to read more or reply. They are not designers — they are evaluating competence and personality. Speed of comprehension is everything. No one is browsing leisurely.

### Brand Personality
**Sharp, grounded, curious.**
- Sharp: precise language, no filler words, no vague claims. Every line earns its place.
- Grounded: rooted in real work (agri supply chain, field visits, actual tools built). Not aspirational fluff.
- Curious: the AI certs, the unusual background (chemical engineering → PM), the specific projects — signals someone who follows threads.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light. These are professional day-context readers, not developers in dark offices.
- **Inspiration**: Karpathy (substance-first, zero decoration), Paul Graham (pure text authority), Lilian Weng (quiet confidence). Content IS the design.
- **Anti-reference**: AI-slop portfolios — gradient hero sections, glassmorphism cards, neon accents, "I'm a passionate innovator" copy. Over-designed templates that perform creativity without showing any.
- **Visual tone**: Monochrome with intention. White space is the luxury, not color. Typography does all the heavy lifting.

### The One Thing
**"I solve problems with tech, agnostic to everything else."** The site should make a recruiter feel they've met someone who thinks clearly, builds real things, and doesn't waste words. Not impressive-seeming — *convincing*.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over cleverness.** If a visitor has to think about what something means, rewrite it.
2. **Evidence over claims.** Every skill label needs a concrete anchor. "Stakeholder management" means nothing; "aligned ops, tech, and business at Farmart" means something.
3. **Restraint as signal.** What's removed is as important as what's kept. A sparse page from someone who could have added more signals taste and confidence.
4. **Typography carries the weight.** With a monochrome palette, font size, weight, and spacing are the only tools. Use them with precision.
5. **Fast comprehension.** A recruiter skimming for 30 seconds should be able to answer: who is this, what do they do, should I reply?
